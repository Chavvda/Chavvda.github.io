Campfire

Hackathon Finalist

Campfire is a community based story sharing platfrom. It gives everyone equal oppurtunities to voice their opinions and help people connect by giving them a deeper understanding of one another

Research / UI / UX

Challenge

“How might we encourage elders to connect and socialize”

Problem

The lack of connection in communities causes Social Isolation amongst older adults

Solution

Help local communities develop stronger connections by giving people the opportunity to share stories and experiences amongst one another.

It provides many accessibility options for the visually or physically impaired.

The Campfire application will allow people to share stories and experiences amongst the local population by utilizing the users geo location

Concept

Campfire is an experience for older adults that makes it easier for them to connect to one another by sharing experiences and stories to the people living in the same area using the location. It also allows the people to view the story live and comment as well. We also got an opportunity to interview seniors and get feedback as well

Target Audience

Older Adults

Ideation

We wanted to create a solution which would help older adults connect with the society without the need to travel, message, etc. To get to know different people living nearby, making new connections just by sharing their experiences

Pain Points

Movement

Difficult to type

Difficulty to read

Encouragement to communicate

Accessibility to connect with others, being in social setting

Experience

Test experiencing of how some older adults feel by tying weights on our ankles, back, hands and wearing yellow tinted glasses to simulate vision.


Mobility is limited on arms, legs, back

Ability to move fingers resulted into slow time consuming movement

Blurred/Faded vision made it difficult to read, which resulted to reading words incorrectly.

Causes miscommunication

Accessibility

Ability to adjust font size

Using voice recording to comment

High Contrast

Colorblind Settings also suitable color scheme suiting aging eyes

Technology

Google Home Mini

The user will have the ability to request specific user’s stories to be played as well as using the Google Home Mini to record their story. This will allow the user to utilize the application verbally instead of physically

Computer Vision

When the user wants to create a video they will have the option to record themselves as the speak. Computer vision will detect the user and create a frame for the user to begin recording a video

Google Maps Api

Utilizing the Google Maps Api in order to track the location of the user in order to display results by location

Navigation

Ability to adjust font size

Using voice recording to comment

High Contrast

Colorblind Settings, Suitable color scheme suiting aging eyes

Design

Logo

Resembling the coziness of a camp fire where individuals gather and tell stories

Color

Color scheme that is meant to be the most comfortable for older adult's vision

Font

Font is bold, showing contrast and they have the ability to increase font size

Home Page

It allows the user to view the stories from location and the themes they’ve been listening more often.

Clicking the button at the bottom would allow them to start recording the story.

Navigation

Quick navigation to home, profile, friends and settings.

Notifications also in place and highlighted at the top so they may not miss what is the most important (sharing stories and having conversation).

Recorded Stories

In case the individual doesn't prefer reading, or unable to read for the most part.

Visual representation of the spot they are in the recording, while also showing the time people have commented.

Reflection

Creating a design that is meant for older adult's vision, hearing and focusing on other accessibility features that is needed (Font size)

Narrow down the user experience, get some user testing to determine how to make it simpler

Gained knowledge and empathy for older adults because of the conversation with them and how we may implement these accessibilities in other apps or website to accommodate them
